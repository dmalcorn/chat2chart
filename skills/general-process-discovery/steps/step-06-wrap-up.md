# Step 6: Wrap-Up and Confirmation

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES:
- ALWAYS do a final walkthrough summary before closing
- Give the interviewee a chance to add anything they forgot
- Thank them genuinely — their time and knowledge are valuable
- End on a warm note — this person may be interviewed again for follow-ups

## YOUR TASK:
Summarize the entire discovered process, check for gaps, give the interviewee a final chance to add or correct anything, and close the interview warmly.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL:

### Final Summary
Present a concise walkthrough of the entire process as captured:
"Let me walk through everything we've covered to make sure I have the full picture..."

Summarize:
1. The trigger — what starts the process
2. The main flow — confirmed steps in sequence
3. Key decision points — where the process branches
4. Notable exceptions — workarounds, error recovery, escalation
5. Key systems and handoffs — tools used, people involved

### Gap Check
After the summary, ask:
- "Is there anything I missed or got wrong?"
- "Is there anything about this process that we didn't cover that you think is important?"
- "Is there a step that happens before or after what we discussed?"

### Closing
If the interviewee has nothing to add:
- Thank them: "This has been really helpful. You've given me a clear picture of how [process] actually works."
- Set expectations if applicable: "Your description will be combined with others to build a complete picture of this workflow."
- End warmly: "Thanks again for your time!"

## SUCCESS METRICS:
- Final summary covers all confirmed steps, decisions, exceptions, and handoffs
- Interviewee confirms the summary is accurate or provides corrections
- Interviewee had an explicit opportunity to add forgotten details
- Interview ends with the interviewee feeling valued and heard

## FAILURE MODES:
- Ending abruptly without a final summary
- Skipping the gap check — interviewees often remember something at the end
- Summarizing at too high a level, missing confirmed details
- Ending on a transactional note rather than a warm one
- Not giving the interviewee enough time to think about gaps
