# Step 3: Decision Points

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES:
- REFLECT AND CONFIRM every decision branch before exploring the next
- Reference specific steps from the walkthrough — "You mentioned earlier that you [step]. What happens when...?"
- Explore BOTH sides of every decision — the "yes" path and the "no" path
- If the interviewee already covered a decision point during the walkthrough, confirm and move on

## YOUR TASK:
Circle back through the confirmed process steps and probe for conditional branches, choices, and decision points. For each decision, capture what triggers it, what the options are, who decides, and what happens on each branch.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL:

### Identify Decision Points
Review the confirmed steps from Step 2. Look for:
- Steps the interviewee flagged as conditional ("it depends on...", "unless...", "if...")
- Steps that seem like they could have alternatives (approvals, classifications, routing)
- Steps where the interviewee used hedge language ("usually", "most of the time", "typically")

### Probe Each Decision
For each identified decision point:
1. Reference the specific step: "You mentioned [step]. You said 'it depends on [condition]'..."
2. Ask about the condition: "How do you decide which way to go?"
3. Explore each branch: "What happens when [condition A]?" then "And when [condition B]?"
4. Check for additional branches: "Is there ever a third option?"
5. Distill and confirm the decision structure

### Look for Hidden Decisions
After covering flagged decision points, ask:
- "Are there any steps where you have to make a judgment call — where it's not obvious what to do next?"
- "Are there steps where different people might do it differently?"

## SUCCESS METRICS:
- Every decision point identified during the walkthrough has been explored
- Both/all branches of each decision are captured with confirmed summaries
- Decision criteria are explicit — "who decides" and "based on what"
- At least one hidden decision point has been probed for

## FAILURE MODES:
- Asking about decisions in the abstract ("Do you have any decision points?") instead of referencing specific steps
- Exploring only the primary branch and not asking about alternatives
- Accepting vague criteria ("it depends") without probing for specifics
- Re-asking about decisions already fully explored during the walkthrough
