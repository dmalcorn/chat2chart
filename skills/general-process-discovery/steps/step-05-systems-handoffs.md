# Step 5: Systems and Handoffs

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES:
- REFLECT AND CONFIRM every system interaction and handoff before probing the next
- Reference specific steps — "You mentioned [step] uses [system]. Tell me more about that."
- Distinguish between systems the interviewee uses directly and systems they interact with indirectly
- Capture handoffs with enough detail to understand the information flow

## YOUR TASK:
Fill in the gaps around tools, systems, documents, and handoffs between people. The walkthrough and probing phases captured what happens and when; this phase captures with what and with whom.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL:

### Systems and Tools
For each confirmed step, check if the system/tool is captured. If not:
- "What system or tool do you use for [step]?"
- "Is that done in software, on paper, or verbally?"
- "What information do you enter or look up in [system]?"

For each system mentioned:
1. What do they do in it? "What specifically do you do in [system] at this point?"
2. What's the input? "What information do you need to have before you use [system]?"
3. What's the output? "What does [system] give you back? A confirmation, a report, a number?"
4. What's the fallback? "What do you do if [system] is down or unavailable?"

### Handoffs Between People
For each step that involves another person or team:
1. Who? "Who specifically receives this? A person, a role, a team?"
2. How? "How do they know it's their turn? Email, notification, verbal, they just check?"
3. What information? "What do you pass along? What do they need to know?"
4. What gets lost? "Is there anything that gets lost or miscommunicated in this handoff?"
5. Distill and confirm the handoff

### Look for Missing Actors
- "Is there anyone else involved in this process we haven't talked about?"
- "Does anyone review or approve what you've done, even informally?"
- "Who would notice if you did this step wrong?"

## SUCCESS METRICS:
- Every step has an identified system/tool or explicit "none — done manually/verbally"
- Every handoff has: who, how they're notified, what information transfers
- Fallback behavior captured for critical systems
- All actors in the process identified by role

## FAILURE MODES:
- Accepting "I use the computer" without asking which system specifically
- Capturing handoffs as one-directional without asking about the receiving side
- Missing informal communication channels (chat, walking over, phone calls)
- Not asking about the fallback when a system is unavailable
