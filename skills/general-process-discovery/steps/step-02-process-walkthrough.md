# Step 2: Process Walkthrough

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES:
- LET THE INTERVIEWEE LEAD — follow their natural narrative, do not impose a structure
- REFLECT AND CONFIRM every substantive response before asking the next question
- ONE QUESTION AT A TIME — never stack multiple questions
- Do not probe deeply for exceptions or decision branches yet — that comes in Steps 3-4

## YOUR TASK:
Get the interviewee to walk through their process from beginning to end in their own words. Your job is to capture the main sequence of steps — the "happy path" — with verified reflective summaries for each step. Save detailed probing for later phases.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL:

### Listen and Distill
After each substantive response, create a reflective summary:
- **What happens:** [action] on [object] by [actor]
- **Why:** [purpose or trigger]
- **How:** [method, system, or tool used]
- **Then:** [what happens next, or handoff]

Confirm: "Did I get that right, or would you adjust anything?"

### Follow the Thread
After confirmation, prompt for the next step:
- "What happens after that?"
- "Then what do you do?"
- "And once that's done...?"

### Note Gaps for Later
If the interviewee mentions a decision point ("it depends on..."), a handoff ("then I send it to..."), or an exception ("unless..."), note it mentally but don't drill into it yet. Acknowledge briefly: "Got it — I'll want to come back to that. For now, what happens next in the typical flow?"

### Detect the End
When the process reaches its natural conclusion, confirm the full sequence:
"So if I'm following correctly, the main flow is: [summarize all confirmed steps in sequence]. Does that capture the typical path from start to finish?"

## SUCCESS METRICS:
- Every process step has a confirmed reflective summary
- The main sequence is captured from trigger to completion
- Decision points and exceptions are noted for later probing, not skipped
- The interviewee's own vocabulary is used, not formal process terminology
- The interviewee feels heard — their expertise is acknowledged

## FAILURE MODES:
- Imposing a structure or asking leading questions ("Do you then verify...?")
- Probing deeply into exceptions before capturing the main flow
- Stacking questions: "What happens next? And who does that? And what system?"
- Moving on without confirming the reflective summary
- Using process engineering jargon the interviewee didn't introduce
- Interrupting the interviewee's narrative to ask about a detail
