# Step 4: Exceptions and Edge Cases

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES:
- REFLECT AND CONFIRM every exception path before probing the next
- Normalize exceptions — "This is really valuable, these unusual situations are exactly what we need to capture"
- Do not judge workarounds or informal practices — they exist for reasons
- Probe explicitly for undocumented practices

## YOUR TASK:
Discover what happens when things go wrong, when the process deviates from the typical flow, and when workers use informal practices or workarounds. Exception paths are often the most valuable discoveries because they reveal gaps between documented procedures and actual work.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL:

### Probe for Known Exceptions
Reference confirmed steps and ask:
- "You described [step]. What happens when that doesn't work as expected?"
- "Has there ever been a time when [step] went differently than usual?"
- "What's the most unusual version of [step] you've experienced?"

### Probe for Workarounds
Ask directly about informal practices:
- "Is there anything you do that isn't in the official procedure but makes things work better?"
- "Are there any shortcuts or workarounds you've developed over time?"
- "What do you do when the system isn't working or isn't available?"

### Probe for Error Recovery
For each exception identified:
1. What triggers it? "How do you know when this is happening?"
2. What do you do? "Walk me through what happens when [exception occurs]"
3. Who else gets involved? "Do you need to notify anyone or escalate?"
4. How does it get back on track? "How does the process resume after this?"
5. Distill and confirm

### Probe for Escalation
- "When do you need to get someone else involved?"
- "What happens when you're not sure what to do?"
- "Who do you go to when things get complicated?"

## SUCCESS METRICS:
- At least one exception or workaround identified for the overall process
- Exception paths captured with the same level of detail as the main flow
- Workarounds and informal practices captured without judgment
- Escalation paths identified — who, when, what information they need

## FAILURE MODES:
- Accepting "it always works" without probing — every process has exceptions
- Judging or evaluating workarounds ("that seems inefficient")
- Treating exceptions as less important than the main flow
- Asking about exceptions in the abstract instead of referencing specific steps
- Missing the transition from exception probing to systems/handoffs
